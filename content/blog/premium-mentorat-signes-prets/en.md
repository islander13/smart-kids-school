We're often asked the question the other way around: "my child is talented, should they move to Premium?" The real question isn't skill level, it's something else: has your child already outgrown a classic class format.

## It's not about level, it's about the spark

Our [Solo](/en/tarifs) plan (from 249 CHF/month) teaches coding: a teacher trained in our method, a structured programme, step-by-step progression. It's the right choice for the vast majority of children, including fast learners. Premium (999 CHF/month) is different in nature, not just intensity: it's direct mentoring with the founder, an EPFL engineer, designed for the child who already has that spark and wants to build something real over time.

## Sign 1: they already code on their own, unprompted

The most reliable signal isn't what happens during a session, it's what happens outside of it: a child who opens Scratch or Python on a random afternoon without being asked, just to try out an idea. It's not an assignment they were given, it's something coming from them.

## Sign 2: they ask questions you can no longer answer

Not "how do I make a website", but sharper, harder-to-dodge questions: "why does my game slow down when there are lots of objects on screen", "how do real developers organise their code". This kind of question signals curiosity that goes beyond a standard programme.

## Sign 3: they have a project idea bigger than what they can currently build

Many advanced children carry "an idea" — an app, a game, a website — that they can't yet build alone, but have been thinking about for a while. That's exactly what Premium mentoring is designed to support: turning that idea into a real, published project over 12 months.

## Sign 4: they enjoy competing, not just progressing

Some children are motivated by pushing themselves further, not just by steady progress: entering a competition, aiming for a visible result. Premium includes preparation for Swiss computing olympiads and junior hackathons for children interested in that format — not a requirement, but an option that resonates with certain profiles.

## Sign 5: you, as a parent, are ready to be involved in the follow-up

Premium includes quarterly check-ins with parents, not just with the child. It's designed for families who want to follow a real trajectory — orientation, competitions, projects — not just receive an end-of-month report.

## Not sure yet?

It's normal not to know before trying. A 30-minute initial diagnostic call with the child and parents lets us assess together whether Premium is the right fit — and if it isn't, we'll tell you honestly and point you toward [Solo](/en/tarifs), which remains the excellent choice for most children. Premium also includes a first-month satisfaction guarantee, no questions asked.
