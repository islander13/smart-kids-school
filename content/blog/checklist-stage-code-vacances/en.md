Your child is signed up for a coding camp during the holidays, and the date is coming up: here's the checklist we give families ourselves, so everything starts smoothly from the very first half-day.

## Equipment, to check the night before

- **A computer, not a tablet or smartphone.** A PC, Mac or Chromebook all work, as long as a physical keyboard and a real browser are available. Programming, even simplified with Scratch, doesn't work well with a finger on a touchscreen.
- **A stable internet connection.** If possible, plug the computer in via cable rather than Wi-Fi during camp week, or at least check the Wi-Fi signal is good in the room being used.
- **Headphones.** Essential to hear the teacher clearly and avoid the sound disturbing the rest of the house.
- **A webcam**, ideally, so the teacher can see the child in addition to their screen — not required, but it helps build a real connection.

Best practice: turn the computer on the evening before and check that everything works (sound, mic, camera), rather than discovering a problem 5 minutes before camp starts.

## The environment, on the day

A child focuses better in a calm space, without a TV on in the background or constant foot traffic. It doesn't need to be a real desk: the kitchen or living room table works fine, as long as the spot stays the same for the whole week (switching rooms every day disrupts their bearings).

## Talking with your child before day one

Two or three minutes is enough to:
- Remind them of the chosen theme (video game, robot, or Python), so they arrive with an idea of what to expect.
- Reassure them: no prerequisites are needed, the camp is designed for complete beginners as much as for children who've already practiced a bit.
- Explain the pace: 3-hour half-days, with regular breaks, not one continuous lecture.

## On the day

- Log in 5 to 10 minutes early, to leave time to fix any technical hiccup without stress.
- Have something to drink within reach.
- Have a parent reachable in case of a technical question, especially on the first day — no need to stay in the room the whole time.

## What if there's a technical issue on the day?

It happens, and it's not a big deal: contact us directly (via WhatsApp or email), our team helps fix it quickly, or reschedules the session if needed. It's never a reason to miss the whole camp.
